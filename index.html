The Critical Thinking Framework

Developed by Dr. Fahim Ahmed at the University of Strathclyde, the Critical Thinking Framework (CTF) revolutionizes mathematics education by focusing on practical classroom implementation rather than theoretical taxonomies.

Through years of research and student feedback, CTF identifies four key components that enhance student engagement and learning outcomes:

Strategic Questions

Thought-provoking questions placed at key lecture stages to stimulate critical thinking and connect concepts to prior knowledge.

Open Case Discussion

Real-world examples linked to student experiences, encouraging collaborative problem-solving and practical application.

Collaborative Feedback

Live assessment reviews where students connect coursework to topics, identifying strengths and areas for improvement.

Active Learning

Sensory simulations and emotional intelligence monitoring to maintain attention and create an engaging environment.

Why CTF Matters

The CTF addresses a critical gap in mathematics education - the disconnect between theoretical frameworks and practical classroom implementation. Traditional approaches often overwhelm educators with complex pedagogical models, while CTF offers:

  • A practical toolkit that integrates seamlessly into existing curricula
  • Evidence-based strategies that improve student engagement and outcomes
  • Flexible components that adapt to diverse learning environments
  • A focus on creating inclusive classrooms where all students participate

Research shows that CTF implementation leads to a 53% increase in student engagement and a 61% improvement in concept understanding based on student surveys.

Mathematics Educators Training

The Mathematics Educators Training (MET) program translates the Critical Thinking Framework into practical strategies for Scottish mathematics teachers. Designed specifically for higher primary (P5-P7) and secondary educators, MET combines CTF principles with Scotland's Curriculum for Excellence.

Interactive Sessions

2-hour weekly face-to-face workshops complemented by preparation and reflection activities

Technology Integration

Mentimeter, Wacom tablets, and digital tools to enhance engagement

Case-Based Learning

Real classroom scenarios from Scottish schools with practical solutions

Program Structure

Pre-Session (2 Hours)

  • Implement CTF strategy
  • Collect student feedback
  • Prepare demonstration materials

Face-to-Face (2 Hours)

  • Share challenges & solutions
  • Teacher demonstrations
  • Vote & refine best strategies

Post-Session (2 Hours)

  • Reflect on implementation
  • Apply "best strategy"
  • Design next case study
Certification & Recognition

Upon successful completion, participants receive:

  • Scottish Government-recognized certification
  • Professional development credits
  • Membership in the MET educator network
  • Digital badge for professional profiles
