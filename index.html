Program Overview

The Mathematics Educators Training (MET) program is designed to transform mathematics teaching in Scottish higher primary (P5-P7) and secondary schools. Based on Fahim Ahmed's Critical Thinking Framework (CTF), this 8-week intensive program empowers teachers with student-centric strategies, innovative technology, and collaborative approaches.

Duration & Format
  • 8 weeks of intensive training
  • 2-hour weekly face-to-face sessions
  • 2 hours pre-session preparation
  • 2 hours post-session reflection
  • Access to exclusive MET digital platform
Core Objectives
  • Implement Critical Thinking Framework in math education
  • Develop student-centric teaching approaches
  • Integrate technology for enhanced engagement
  • Create inclusive learning environments
  • Build collaborative teacher communities
Certification
  • Scottish Government-recognized certification
  • Professional development credits
  • Ongoing access to MET resources
  • Membership in the MET educator network
  • Digital badge for professional profiles

Weekly Session Structure

Pre-Session (2 Hours)

  • Implement CTF strategy in classroom
  • Collect student feedback via Mentimeter
  • Analyze student challenges and responses
  • Prepare demonstration materials
  • Upload materials to MET platform

Face-to-Face Session (2 Hours)

  • Review student poll results (10 min)
  • Share challenges & attempted solutions (20 min)
  • Teacher demonstrations (40 min)
  • Vote & refine best strategies (20 min)
  • Focus on student voice & engagement (20 min)
  • Assign next week's pre-work (10 min)

Post-Session (2 Hours)

  • Reflect on implementation in journal
  • Apply voted "best strategy" in classroom
  • Design next week's case study
  • Share outcomes on MET platform
  • Engage in peer feedback discussions
